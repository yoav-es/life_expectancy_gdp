Introduction¶

The goal of this project is to investigate whether there is a connection between life expectancy and GDP across

different countries and over time.

Specifically, we want to answer: If GDP rises, does life expectancy change accordingly? If so, how?

GDP (Gross Domestic Product) is the total monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country's

borders in a specific time period. It’s an indicator of a nation’s economic health and performance. In this dataset,

GDP values are given in US dollars (trillions).

We will analyze data from six countries between 2000 and 2015 to explore this dataset

# Create 3x2 grid
fig, axes = plt.subplots(3, 2, figsize=(14, 12))
fig.suptitle('Life Expectancy and GDP Over Time', fontsize=16)
axes = axes.flatten()

# Loop through countries and plot
for i, country in enumerate(countries):
    data = df[df['country'] == country]
    ax1 = axes[i]
    ax2 = ax1.twinx()

    # Plot using matplotlib for better control
    line1, = ax1.plot(data['year'], data['life_expectancy'], color='blue', label='Life Expectancy')
    line2, = ax2.plot(data['year'], data['gdp'], color='orange', label='GDP')

    # Add combined legend to ax1
    ax1.legend(handles=[line1, line2], loc='upper left', fontsize='small')

    # Titles and labels
    ax1.set_title(country)
    ax1.set_xlabel('Year')
    ax1.set_ylabel('Life Expectancy', color='blue')
    ax2.set_ylabel('GDP', color='orange')

# Tidy up layout
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Conclusions¶

By analyzing various aspects of the data—life expectancy over time, GDP over time, their relationship by country,

and the distribution of each variable—we observed the following:

  • In every country, both GDP and life expectancy have increased over the 15-year period.

  • The GDP and life expectancy graph reveals a clear positive correlation across countries, supported by a Pearson correlation coefficient of 0.34

  • The average life expectancy is around 72 years, with most countries exceeding this figure except Zimbabwe.

  • The GDP distribution is highly right-skewed, indicating a power-law pattern where a small number of countries have very high GDP.

  • Life expectancy distribution is left-skewed, suggesting that most countries have relatively high life expectancy, with zimbabwe as an outlier with under the average score.
